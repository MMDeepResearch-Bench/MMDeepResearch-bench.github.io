Introduction

MMDR-Bench targets end-to-end multimodal deep research: models must interpret task-provided images, gather supporting sources, and synthesize long-form reports with verifiable Claim‚ÄìURL citations, while staying faithful to visual evidence.

Start with Dataset to see what tasks look like, then use the Leaderboard section to compare systems by overall score and per-metric trade-offs.

Dataset

MMDR-Bench Benchmark

MMDR-Bench contains 140 expert-crafted tasks across 21 domains, organized into two complementary regimes: Daily (screenshots/photos/UI captures) and Research (charts/diagrams/tables requiring longer synthesis).

Each instance is packaged as an image‚Äìtext bundle with a variable number of images. We annotate difficulty and record task language, enabling evaluation in realistic settings.

Experiments

Results & Findings

Results highlight cross-metric trade-offs between writing quality, citation discipline, and multimodal grounding. Use the leaderboard below to sort models and compare per-metric performance.

Key Findings

1) Vision helps only when it is reliable as evidence. Noisy or auxiliary visuals can seed incorrect premises that propagate through retrieval and synthesis.

2) Multimodal alignment and citation grounding can diverge. Better visual grounding does not automatically imply stronger claim‚ÄìURL support.

3) Tool use helps, but strong backbones and richer retrieval matter most. Agents can amplify backbones, but cannot replace them.
